# Post-Market Surveillance Standard

## Scope

This standard governs intake, deduplication, triage, investigation, trend detection, protective restrictions, regulatory-reporting assessment, corrective action, effectiveness review and closure for complaints, incidents, quality signals, safety reports, supplier notifications, returns and public-source signals.

## Severity model

- LOW — isolated, non-serious, no credible product or batch risk.
- MODERATE — recurring or material quality/service issue requiring investigation.
- HIGH — credible safety, quality, traceability or misuse risk requiring prompt restriction and senior review.
- CRITICAL — serious or potentially widespread risk requiring immediate protective controls, incident command and regulatory-reporting assessment.

## Critical controls

- Serious and critical signals require product/passport/batch traceability or a documented reason why traceability is unavailable.
- Critical cases remain open until investigation, corrective action, reporting assessment and effectiveness review are complete.
- Automation may aggregate and escalate but cannot make a clinical diagnosis or close a case.
- Customer identifiers in analytics outputs must be pseudonymised.
- Signal thresholds do not replace professional judgement.

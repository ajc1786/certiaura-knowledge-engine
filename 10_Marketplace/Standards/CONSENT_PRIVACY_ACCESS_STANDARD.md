# Consent, Privacy and Access Standard

## Principles

1. Purpose limitation and data minimisation.
2. Current privacy notice and recorded legal basis.
3. Consent recorded only where consent is the appropriate basis; consent is not inferred from inactivity.
4. Least-privilege role-based access.
5. Multi-factor authentication where the access profile requires it.
6. Retention and deletion controls linked to a defined schedule.
7. Rights requests are identity-checked, logged, reviewed and closed by a named human.
8. Sensitive data is pseudonymised or segregated where practicable.
9. Automated tools may route and flag a request but may not decide legal exemptions or close the request.

## Rights-request states

`RECEIVED → IDENTITY_CHECK → ASSESSMENT → ACTION_IN_PROGRESS → COMPLETED | REFUSED_WITH_REASONS`

Any refusal or partial response requires a legal or privacy review record and appeal/escalation route.

# BPC-157 CLINICAL TRANSLATION GAP

## Preclinical evidence now directly strengthened

### Pharmacokinetics / ADME

Direct animal evidence shows:

- rapid peak concentrations;
- short prototype half-life;
- species-dependent intramuscular bioavailability;
- rapid metabolism to smaller peptide fragments.

### Tendon fibroblast signalling

Direct in-vitro rat evidence supports:

- increased growth hormone receptor expression;
- altered growth-hormone / JAK2-related signalling.

### Angiogenic signalling

Direct primary evidence supports:

- VEGFR2-related pro-angiogenic signalling in experimental systems.

## The translation gap

None of the above independently establishes:

- clinically meaningful human tissue repair;
- a validated human injection dose;
- a validated treatment duration;
- superiority to rehabilitation, surgery or approved therapy;
- long-term human safety.

## Key Certiaura conclusion

BPC-157 has **biological plausibility and a substantial preclinical research base**.

The principal missing layer is **robust human clinical validation**.

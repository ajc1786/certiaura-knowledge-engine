
# Retatrutide evidence corpus and scientific review baseline

## Executive position

Retatrutide is a once-weekly investigational triple hormone-receptor agonist that activates glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide (GIP), glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) and glucagon receptors. The evidence base now includes translational discovery work, phase 1b and phase 2 trials, a published phase 3 type 2 diabetes trial, mechanistic structural evidence, body-composition and liver-fat substudies, phase 3 programme design evidence, trial-registry records and sponsor-reported phase 3 obesity topline results.

The evidence is promising but must remain explicitly separated by source maturity. Peer-reviewed trial results, official trial-registry records and sponsor topline releases are not interchangeable. Retatrutide remains investigational and must not be represented as approved, publicly available, appropriate for self-administration or suitable for an individual.

## Controlled conclusions

1. **Mechanism:** Primary translational and structural evidence supports triple agonism at GIP, GLP-1 and glucagon receptors.
2. **Obesity:** The phase 2 obesity trial reported approximately 24.2% mean weight reduction at 48 weeks in the 12 mg group. Sponsor-reported TRIUMPH-1 topline results reported 28.3% at 80 weeks at 12 mg, but the topline source remains conditional pending a full peer-reviewed publication and regulator assessment.
3. **Type 2 diabetes:** Published phase 2 and phase 3 evidence supports glycaemic and bodyweight effects in the studied trial populations.
4. **Liver fat and body composition:** Substudies support liver-fat and total-fat-mass reductions, with important endpoint and attrition limitations.
5. **Safety:** Gastrointestinal adverse events are the most consistent tolerability finding. Heart-rate increases were observed in phase 2 obesity data. Long-term outcome and safety questions remain active.
6. **Regulatory status:** The compound is investigational and is not approved for public use as of this baseline.

## Publication controls

- Use the claim-evidence matrix for all public statements.
- Preserve the source label: peer reviewed, registry, or official non-peer-reviewed disclosure.
- Include the investigational-status notice wherever efficacy, safety, availability or access is discussed.
- Do not generate personal dosing, reconstitution, administration or purchasing instructions from this evidence baseline.
- Re-run scientific review when a surveillance trigger fires.

## Study scope

Preclinical pharmacokinetic, distribution, metabolism and excretion research in:

- Sprague-Dawley rats
- Beagle dogs

## Rat pharmacokinetics

### Intravenous

- Dose: **20 μg/kg**
- Mean elimination half-life: **15.2 minutes**

## Additional findings

- Prototype BPC-157 was reported as not detectable four hours after administration in the studied animal experiments.
- Prototype elimination half-life was less than 30 minutes across the reported conditions.
- Urine and bile were reported as major excretory pathways.
- Radiolabel work indicated rapid metabolism into smaller peptide fragments and amino acids.

## Critical human-translation control

The paper discusses a **proposed clinical dose** and uses body-surface-area conversions to design animal experiments.

Certiaura does **not** treat this as a validated human dosing protocol.

Animal PK cannot establish:

- a validated human dose;
- a validated injection frequency;
- a validated treatment cycle;
- a validated route for a particular human condition.

Human pharmacokinetic and clinical evidence would be required.

## Limitations

- No human pharmacokinetic participants.
- Direct species-to-human dosing translation is not valid.
- Tissue metabolite analysis was incomplete.
- Some analyses used small animal sample sizes.

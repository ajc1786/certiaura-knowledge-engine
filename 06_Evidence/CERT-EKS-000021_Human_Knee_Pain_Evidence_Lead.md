## Publication identity

**Title:** Intra-Articular Injection of BPC 157 for Multiple Types of Knee Pain  
**Journal:** Alternative Therapies in Health and Medicine  
**Year:** 2021

## Human scope currently supported by secondary reporting

- Approximate study size reported: **12 patients**
- Intervention character: intra-articular BPC-157 injection
- Population character: chronic / multiple forms of knee pain

## Important outcome discrepancy

Two contemporary secondary summaries do not use the same headline outcome:

### Secondary summary A

Reports that **7 of 12** patients experienced relief lasting more than six months.

Source:
https://www.menshealth.com/fitness/a70398841/bpc-157-what-to-know-now/

### Secondary summary B

Reports improvement in **11 of 12** subjects and separately criticises the lack of validated survey tools.

Source:
https://nypost.com/2025/06/05/health/joe-rogan-among-the-fans-of-revolutionary-anti-aging-peptide/

## Certiaura decision

Neither shorthand figure is promoted as the definitive primary-study result.

The discrepancy itself is now part of the evidence record.

## Risk-of-bias position

The current evidence base does not establish:

- adequate randomisation;
- an adequate control group;
- blinding;
- validated outcome measurement;
- sufficient sample size;
- long-term safety.

## Evidence weight

**Very low**

## Permitted wording

> A very small human knee-pain report has been published, but the evidence is uncontrolled / methodologically weak and does not establish confirmatory efficacy.

## Not permitted

- “BPC-157 is proven to treat knee pain.”
- “BPC-157 repairs human knee tissue.”
- “This study validates a universal intra-articular dose.”
- “This study proves long-term safety.”

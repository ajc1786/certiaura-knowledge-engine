## Primary source

**Title:** Therapeutic potential of pro-angiogenic BPC157 is associated with VEGFR2 activation and up-regulation

**Journal:** Journal of Molecular Medicine  
**Published:** 15 November 2016  
**Volume / pages:** 95, 323–333  
**DOI:** `10.1007/s00109-016-1488-y`  
**Primary source:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00109-016-1488-y

## Experimental systems reported

- Chick chorioallantoic membrane assay
- Endothelial tube-formation assay
- Rat hind-limb ischemia model
- Human vascular endothelial cells in vitro

## Key findings from the directly accessed primary abstract

- Increased vessel density was reported in the CAM assay.
- Increased endothelial tube formation was reported in vitro.
- Faster blood-flow recovery and increased vessel number were reported in rat hind-limb ischemia.
- BPC-157 increased VEGFR2 mRNA and protein expression in human vascular endothelial cells in vitro.
- VEGF-A expression was not reported as increased in that experiment.
- The study reported VEGFR2 internalization and activation of the VEGFR2–Akt–eNOS signalling pathway.

## Interpretation

This provides a direct primary basis for a **pro-angiogenic / vascular-signalling mechanism**.

## Critical boundaries

This does not establish:

- human injury-healing efficacy;
- a clinical cardiovascular benefit;
- a validated human dose;
- a causal cancer risk.

The correct Certiaura interpretation is:

> A mechanistic angiogenesis signal exists in preclinical and in-vitro experimental systems; long-term human consequences remain unresolved.

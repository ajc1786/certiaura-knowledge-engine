## Primary source

Rosenstock J, Frias J, Jastreboff AM, et al.  
**Retatrutide, a GIP, GLP-1 and glucagon receptor agonist, for people with type 2 diabetes: a randomised, double-blind, placebo and active-controlled, parallel-group, phase 2 trial conducted in the USA.**  
*The Lancet*. 2023;402(10401):529-544.

**DOI:** `10.1016/S0140-6736(23)01053-X`  
**ClinicalTrials.gov:** `NCT04867785`

## Design

- Phase 2
- Randomized
- Double blind
- Double dummy
- Placebo controlled
- Active comparator: dulaglutide 1.5 mg
- 42 centres in the USA
- 281 randomized participants
- 275 participants in efficacy analyses
- 36-week study duration
- Primary endpoint: change in HbA1c at week 24

## Population

Adults aged 18–75 years with:

- Type 2 diabetes
- HbA1c 7.0–10.5%
- BMI 25–50 kg/m²
- Diet and exercise alone or stable metformin

## Safety evidence

- Mild-to-moderate gastrointestinal adverse events occurred in 35% of participants across retatrutide groups.
- No severe hypoglycaemia was reported.
- No deaths occurred during the study.

## Evidence limitations

- Phase 2 evidence rather than confirmatory phase 3 evidence.
- Study duration was 36 weeks.
- The active comparator was dulaglutide 1.5 mg.
- The study was funded by Eli Lilly and Company.

## Primary source

Jastreboff AM, Kaplan LM, Frias JP, et al.  
**Triple-Hormone-Receptor Agonist Retatrutide for Obesity — A Phase 2 Trial.**  
*New England Journal of Medicine*. 2023.

**DOI:** `10.1056/NEJMoa2301972`  
**ClinicalTrials.gov:** `NCT04881760`

## Design

- Phase 2
- Randomized
- Double blind
- Placebo controlled
- Dose ranging
- 48-week duration
- 338 participants
- Adults with obesity, or overweight with at least one weight-related condition, without diabetes

## Key efficacy evidence

- Weight reduction was dose dependent.
- At 24 weeks, the highest-dose groups produced mean weight reductions around 17%.
- At 48 weeks, mean body-weight reduction in the 12 mg group was **24.2%**, versus **2.1%** with placebo.
- Higher-dose groups had not clearly reached a weight-loss plateau by week 48.

## Safety evidence

- Gastrointestinal adverse events were the most frequent.
- These were generally described as mild to moderate and occurred particularly during dose escalation.
- Heart rate increased in a dose-dependent pattern, peaked around week 24, and subsequently declined.

## Evidence limitations

- This was a phase 2 dose-ranging study, not confirmatory phase 3 evidence.
- The 48-week duration does not establish long-term benefit-risk balance.
- The population excluded people with diabetes.
- Sponsor involvement and declared conflicts should be considered in interpretation.

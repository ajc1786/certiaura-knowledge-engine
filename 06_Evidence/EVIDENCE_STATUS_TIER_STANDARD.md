# EVIDENCE STATUS TIER STANDARD

## Tier A — Peer-reviewed primary evidence

Use when a primary scientific publication has been identified and captured.

Permitted uses:
- Direct evidence synthesis
- Quantitative extraction when verified
- Relationship scoring
- Higher-confidence report generation

## Tier B — Primary sponsor / official topline evidence

Use when:
- the sponsor or trial organisation has released results directly;
- full peer-reviewed publication is not yet captured.

Required label:
**Topline / not yet peer-reviewed**

Permitted uses:
- Development-status tracking
- Provisional outcome summaries
- Safety signal monitoring
- Evidence-gap identification

Not permitted:
- Silent presentation as equivalent to peer-reviewed evidence

## Tier C — Evidence lead pending primary-source capture

Use when a credible secondary source reports a result but the underlying primary source has not yet been captured.

Required label:
**Evidence lead — primary source pending**

Permitted uses:
- Research queue
- Source-discovery workflow
- Provisional watchlist

Not permitted:
- Platinum certification
- Final quantitative synthesis
- Unqualified clinical claims

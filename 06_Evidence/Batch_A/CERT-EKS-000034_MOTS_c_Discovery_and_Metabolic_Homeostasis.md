# CERT-EKS-000034 — MOTS-c Discovery and Metabolic Homeostasis

**Root asset:** `CERT-PKS-000006`  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Status:** CLOSED FOR CORE PRIMARY EXTRACTION  
**Last Review:** 2026-07-16

## Publication

**Title:** The mitochondrial-derived peptide MOTS-c promotes metabolic homeostasis and reduces obesity and insulin resistance  
**Journal:** Cell Metabolism  
**Year:** 2015  
**DOI:** `10.1016/j.cmet.2015.02.009`  
**PMID:** `25738459`

## Study design

Discovery study combining molecular, cell and mouse experiments.

## Population or model

Cell systems and multiple mouse metabolic models; endogenous peptide detection included human samples.

## Intervention or exposure

Experimental MOTS-c exposure in cells and mice; mouse studies included intraperitoneal treatment.

> This is a historical study-design record, not a recommended administration protocol.

## Extracted outcomes

- Identified a mitochondrial 12S-rRNA short open reading frame encoding a 16-amino-acid peptide.
- Mechanistic data implicated folate/purine metabolism and AMPK activation.
- Skeletal muscle was a principal target in the study.
- Mouse treatment prevented age- and high-fat-diet-related insulin resistance and diet-induced obesity in the reported models.
- The evidence was not a human therapeutic intervention trial.

## Limitations

- Predominantly cell and animal evidence.
- Mouse injection data do not establish human dosing.
- No human efficacy or long-term safety conclusion.

## Permitted claims

- MOTS-c is a mitochondrial-derived 16-amino-acid peptide studied in metabolic cell and mouse models.
- The study linked MOTS-c to AMPK-related metabolic signalling.

## Prohibited claims

- Claim that MOTS-c reverses human obesity or diabetes.
- Translation of animal study dosing into human use.

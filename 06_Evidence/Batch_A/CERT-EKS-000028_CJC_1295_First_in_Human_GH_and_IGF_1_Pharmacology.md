# CERT-EKS-000028 — CJC-1295 First-in-Human GH and IGF-1 Pharmacology

**Root asset:** `CERT-PKS-000004`  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Status:** CLOSED FOR PRIMARY ABSTRACT EXTRACTION  
**Last Review:** 2026-07-16

## Publication

**Title:** Prolonged stimulation of growth hormone and insulin-like growth factor I secretion by CJC-1295 in healthy adults  
**Journal:** Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism  
**Year:** 2006  
**DOI:** `10.1210/jc.2005-1536`  
**PMID:** `16352683`

## Study design

Two randomised, placebo-controlled, double-blind ascending-dose trials lasting 28 and 49 days.

## Population or model

Healthy adults aged 21–61 years at two investigational sites.

## Intervention or exposure

Subcutaneous single ascending doses followed by weekly or biweekly repeated-dose cohorts.

> This is a historical study-design record, not a recommended administration protocol.

## Extracted outcomes

- Single-dose CJC-1295 produced dose-dependent mean GH increases of approximately 2- to 10-fold for at least 6 days.
- Mean IGF-1 increased approximately 1.5- to 3-fold for 9–11 days.
- Estimated half-life was 5.8–8.1 days.
- After multiple doses, mean IGF-1 remained above baseline for up to 28 days.
- No serious adverse reactions were reported in these small short-duration trials.

## Limitations

- Healthy volunteer pharmacology, not therapeutic efficacy.
- Small early-phase trials.
- No long-term safety conclusion.
- The studied DAC compound is not modified GRF (1-29).

## Permitted claims

- CJC-1295 with DAC produced sustained GH and IGF-1 elevation in early healthy-adult trials.
- The compound had a multi-day estimated half-life in the study.

## Prohibited claims

- Approved treatment claims.
- Long-term safety claims.
- Transfer of results to non-DAC products or blends.

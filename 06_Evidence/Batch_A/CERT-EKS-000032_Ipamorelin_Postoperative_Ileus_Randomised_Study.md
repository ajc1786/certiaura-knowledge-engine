# CERT-EKS-000032 — Ipamorelin Postoperative Ileus Randomised Study

**Root asset:** `CERT-PKS-000005`

**Status:** CLOSED FOR PRIMARY ABSTRACT EXTRACTION  
**Last Review:** 2026-07-16

## Publication

**Title:** Prospective, randomized, controlled, proof-of-concept study of the ghrelin mimetic ipamorelin for postoperative ileus  
**Journal:** International Journal of Colorectal Disease  
**Year:** 2014  
**DOI:** `10.1007/s00384-014-2030-8`  
**PMID:** `25331030`

## Study design

Multicentre, double-blind, placebo-controlled phase 2 proof-of-concept trial.

## Population or model

Adults undergoing open or laparoscopic small- or large-bowel resection.

## Intervention or exposure

Intravenous 0.03 mg/kg study infusions twice daily from postoperative day 1 through day 7 or discharge.

> This is a historical study-design record, not a recommended administration protocol.

## Extracted outcomes

- 117 patients enrolled; 114 formed the safety and modified intention-to-treat populations.
- Median time to first tolerated standardised meal was 25.3 hours with ipamorelin and 32.6 hours with placebo; p=0.15.
- Treatment-emergent adverse events occurred in 87.5% and 94.8% respectively.
- No significant difference was found in key or secondary efficacy analyses.
- The authors characterised the study regimen as well tolerated in this short inpatient setting.

## Limitations

- Small proof-of-concept study.
- Broad surgical population.
- Primary efficacy endpoint was not statistically significant.
- Short-duration inpatient exposure.

## Permitted claims

- The phase 2 postoperative-ileus trial did not demonstrate significant efficacy over placebo.
- Short study exposure was described as well tolerated.

## Prohibited claims

- Claim that ipamorelin treats postoperative ileus.
- Use of the study regimen as a general dosing protocol.

## Primary source

**Title:** Pentadecapeptide BPC 157 Enhances the Growth Hormone Receptor Expression in Tendon Fibroblasts

**Journal:** Molecules  
**Date:** 19 November 2014  
**DOI:** `10.3390/molecules191119066`  
**Primary source:** https://www.mdpi.com/1420-3049/19/11/19066

## Model

Primary tendon fibroblasts isolated from Achilles tendons of male Sprague-Dawley rats.

## BPC-157 exposure

Reported concentrations:

- 0 μg/mL
- 0.1 μg/mL
- 0.25 μg/mL
- 0.5 μg/mL

Reported exposure periods:

- 1 day
- 2 days
- 3 days

## Key findings

- Growth hormone receptor expression increased in a dose- and time-dependent pattern at mRNA and protein levels.
- The publication reported increases reaching approximately **sevenfold by day 3** in the experimental system.
- Growth hormone added after BPC-157 pretreatment increased viable cell number and PCNA expression.
- Growth hormone stimulation increased phosphorylated JAK2 in BPC-157-pretreated tendon fibroblasts.

## Mechanistic interpretation

The study supports a hypothesis that BPC-157 can alter growth-hormone-receptor-related signalling in **rat tendon fibroblasts in vitro**.

## Critical limitation

This does **not** demonstrate:

- healing of a human tendon;
- a clinical benefit in a patient;
- a validated human dose;
- superiority over rehabilitation or surgery.

The authors themselves note that the in-vitro tendon-fibroblast system cannot reproduce the full in-vivo healing environment.

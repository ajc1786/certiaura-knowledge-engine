## Primary source

Coskun T, Urva S, Roell WC, et al.  
**LY3437943, a novel triple glucagon, GIP, and GLP-1 receptor agonist for glycemic control and weight loss: From discovery to clinical proof of concept.**  
*Cell Metabolism*. 2022;34(9):1234-1247.e9.

**DOI:** `10.1016/j.cmet.2022.07.013`

## Study scope

The publication combines:

- Receptor pharmacology
- Preclinical obesity / metabolic models
- Early human phase 1 single-ascending-dose evidence

## Key evidence captured

- The molecule demonstrated agonist activity at the glucagon, GIP and GLP-1 receptors.
- The preclinical programme linked glucagon receptor activation with increased energy expenditure in the model studied.
- The phase 1 pharmacokinetic profile supported once-weekly dosing.
- The report described body-weight reduction persisting through day 43 after a single dose.

## Safety / tolerability

The authors characterised phase 1 safety and tolerability as broadly similar to other incretin-based therapies.

This should be interpreted as **early-stage evidence**, not long-term safety confirmation.

## Evidence limitations

- The clinical component was phase 1 and not designed to establish long-term efficacy.
- Mechanistic and energy-expenditure findings from animal models are not automatically equivalent to clinical outcome evidence in humans.
- The paper included authors affiliated with Eli Lilly and Company.

## Certiaura interpretation

**Use for:**
- Mechanism
- Receptor-target evidence
- Pharmacokinetic rationale
- Early clinical proof of concept

**Do not use alone for:**
- Long-term efficacy claims
- Long-term safety claims
- Regulatory approval claims

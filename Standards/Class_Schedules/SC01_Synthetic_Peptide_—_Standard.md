# SC01 — Synthetic Peptide — Standard

## Scope

Single defined synthetic peptide without a complex covalent conjugate, heterogeneous biological source or multi-component blend.

## Mandatory evidence

1. Complete amino-acid sequence using one- and three-letter notation
2. N-terminal and C-terminal chemistry
3. Stereochemistry and any D-amino-acid residues
4. Salt, counter-ion, hydration state and calculated molecular mass
5. Manufacturer identity, synthesis site, synthesis route and batch record summary
6. Batch-specific certificate of analysis with numerical results and specifications
7. Orthogonal identity: intact-mass LC-MS plus chromatographic retention comparison
8. Purity and related-peptide impurity profile by validated HPLC/UPLC
9. Peptide content or assay independent of area-percent purity
10. Water content, residual solvents and elemental impurity assessment
11. Reference-standard qualification and traceability
12. Container-closure, storage, transport and stability evidence

## Minimum analytical methods

- LC-MS intact mass
- HPLC or UPLC purity and assay
- Water determination
- Residual-solvent testing

## Automatic rejection or quarantine triggers

- Sequence omitted or inconsistent across documents
- Purity stated without method, chromatogram or numerical result
- Certificate of analysis lacks batch number or manufacturer identity

## Release rule

Completion of this schedule permits Product Passport technical review only. It does not establish approval, safety, efficacy, human-use suitability, direct sale or Platinum status.

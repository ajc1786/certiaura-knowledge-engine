# BPC-157 Source Provenance and Quality Standard

Every evidence item must preserve source authority, title, URL, verification date, retrieval hash where available, evidence tier, jurisdiction, publication or update state, and whether the item is a proposal, registry record, completed result or secondary synthesis.

Commercial supplier pages, testimonials, social content and unsourced summaries cannot establish safety, effectiveness, approval, sport eligibility or product quality. They may only be logged as market-observation inputs and must not drive a transition decision.

A regulatory proposal must remain labelled as a proposal. A recruiting trial without posted results cannot be represented as evidence of efficacy or established safety. Evidence copied from the Tesamorelin pilot is prohibited unless it is a reusable governance control with no peptide-specific claim.

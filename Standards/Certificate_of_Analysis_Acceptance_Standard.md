# CERTIFICATE OF ANALYSIS ACCEPTANCE STANDARD

A supplier certificate of analysis is accepted for review only when it contains:

- manufacturer legal name and manufacturing site;
- product proper name and unambiguous identity;
- batch or lot number;
- manufacture and release dates;
- expiry or retest date;
- sampling date and sampling plan;
- every test performed;
- method reference or method identifier;
- numerical result, unit and acceptance limit;
- identity result based on a specific method;
- quality-unit approval and dated authorised signature;
- document version and controlled-document identifier.

## Mandatory verification

- At least one specific identity test must be independently verified for every received batch before reliance.
- Supplier reliability must be established initially and periodically revalidated.
- Generic, template or undated certificates are not batch evidence.
- “Pass”, “conforms” or “>99% purity” without numerical data and method context is deficient.
- A chromatographic area-percent result does not establish mass content, potency, sterility or absence of endotoxin.

## Immediate rejection

- altered or inconsistent batch identifiers;
- unverifiable laboratory or manufacturer;
- copied chromatograms across batches;
- impossible molecular mass or sequence;
- missing raw-data provenance;
- certificate issued before manufacture or testing;
- results outside specifications presented as conforming.

# DATA INTEGRITY & PROVENANCE STANDARD

Every submitted record must be attributable to a person, laboratory, manufacturer, instrument, method, sample and batch.

## Minimum controls

- original electronic files where available;
- metadata and audit trails preserved;
- no screenshots as substitutes for raw data;
- time-zone and date consistency;
- controlled versions and change history;
- complete result sets, including failures, repeats and invalidated runs;
- documented reason for every repeat or exclusion;
- secure transfer checksum;
- source-language document plus controlled translation where relevant;
- retention period and retrieval commitment.

## Fraud-risk indicators

- identical chromatograms used for different batches;
- cropped or missing axes;
- inconsistent fonts or metadata;
- impossible dates;
- unexplained manual integrations;
- absent failed runs;
- laboratory identity that cannot be independently verified.

Fraud-risk indicators trigger quarantine and escalation.

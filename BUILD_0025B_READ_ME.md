## Critical identity rule

The catalogue provides trade/product names but does not provide:

- amino-acid sequence;
- stereochemistry;
- termini;
- salt or counter-ion;
- source tissue or parent protein;
- molecular formula and mass;
- manufacturer analytical reference standard.

Permanent UAIs are therefore allocated to **supplier product families**, not to fully resolved canonical peptide molecules.

## Evidence-transfer rule

No product may inherit:

- evidence from another short regulatory peptide;
- evidence from a tissue extract with a similar name;
- organ-targeting claims based only on its commercial name;
- anti-ageing or longevity claims from the wider Khavinson peptide literature.

## Regulatory rule

No United States, United Kingdom, European Union or universal medicinal approval claim is created. Any national or regional product status requires direct product- and jurisdiction-specific verification.

## Clinical boundary

No organ-treatment, rejuvenation, longevity, dosing, cycle, injection or reconstitution protocol is created.
